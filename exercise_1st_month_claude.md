# üîÑ Daily Shoulder Prehab (5 minutes)

*Do this EVERY DAY to protect your shoulder and improve pulling strength*

---

## 1. External Rotations
**Reps:** 15 each arm | **Tempo:** Slow and controlled

![External Rotation Exercise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/dumbbell-external-rotation.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand or sit with elbow pinned to your side at 90¬∞
- Hold water bottle in hand
- Rotate forearm outward away from body
- Keep upper arm completely still
- Return slowly to starting position

**Key points:**
- Don't let elbow drift away from body
- Move only at the shoulder joint
- Feel the work in the back of your shoulder

---

## 2. Wall Slides
**Reps:** 10 | **Hold:** 1-2 seconds at top

![Wall Slides Exercise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/wall-slide.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand with back against wall, feet 6 inches away
- Press head, upper back, and lower back into wall
- Place arms in "goalpost" position (elbows at 90¬∞)
- Slide arms up the wall while maintaining contact
- Slide back down to starting position

**Key points:**
- Keep lower back pressed into wall throughout
- If arms come off wall, reduce range of motion
- Feel shoulder blades squeezing together

---

## 3. Shoulder Blade Squeeze (Band Pull-Apart Motion)
**Reps:** 15 | **Hold:** 3 seconds each

![Shoulder Blade Squeeze](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/rear-delt-fly.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand with arms extended forward at shoulder height
- Without bending elbows, pull arms back
- Squeeze shoulder blades together hard
- Hold for 3 seconds
- Return arms forward slowly

**Key points:**
- Imagine squeezing a pencil between shoulder blades
- Keep chest lifted and proud
- Don't shrug shoulders up

---

## 4. Dead Hangs
**Week 1:** 10-15 sec | **Week 4 Goal:** 30 seconds

![Dead Hang Exercise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/dead-hang.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Grip the bar with overhand grip, shoulder-width apart
- Let body hang completely relaxed
- Allow shoulders to elevate naturally (don't actively shrug)
- Keep core slightly engaged to prevent excessive swinging
- Breathe normally throughout

**Key points:**
- Decompresses the spine and shoulders
- Builds grip strength essential for pull-ups
- If 10 seconds is hard, do multiple short hangs

## Week 1 - Full Body Session

### UPPER PUSH

#### Push-Ups
**Sets:** 3 | **Reps:** 10 | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Push-Up Exercise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/push-up.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Start in plank position, hands slightly wider than shoulders
- Body forms a straight line from head to heels
- Lower chest toward floor by bending elbows
- Elbows should track at 45¬∞ angle from body (not flared out)
- Push back up to starting position

**Key points:**
- Keep core tight - no sagging hips
- Touch chest to floor if possible
- Full arm extension at top

---

#### Pike Push-Ups
**Sets:** 2 | **Reps:** 6-8 | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Pike Push-Up](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/pike-push-up.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Start in downward dog position (inverted V shape)
- Hands shoulder-width apart, hips high in the air
- Bend elbows and lower the top of your head toward floor
- Elbows point backward (not out to sides)
- Push back up to inverted V position

**Key points:**
- Targets shoulders more than chest
- Keep legs as straight as possible
- Head should travel in front of hands, not between them

---

### UPPER PULL

#### Dead Hangs
**Sets:** 3 | **Duration:** 15-20 seconds | **Rest:** 45 sec

*(See Prehab section above for form)*

---

#### Negative Pull-Ups
**Sets:** 3 | **Reps:** 3 | **Tempo:** 5-second descent | **Rest:** 90 sec

![Negative Pull-Up](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/jumping-pull-up.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Jump up or step up to the top position (chin above bar)
- Grip bar with overhand grip, shoulder-width or slightly wider
- Engage your lats by pulling shoulder blades down and back
- Slowly lower yourself for 5 full seconds
- Resist gravity - don't just drop
- At bottom, step back up and repeat

**Key points:**
- This builds pull-up strength faster than struggling with full reps
- Count "one-one-thousand, two-one-thousand..." for 5 seconds
- Stop set if you can no longer control the descent

---

#### Inverted Rows (if you have a sturdy table)
**Sets:** 2 | **Reps:** 8 | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Inverted Row](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/inverted-row.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Lie under a sturdy table, face up
- Grip the edge of table with overhand grip
- Body forms a straight line from head to heels
- Pull chest up toward the table edge
- Lower with control

**Key points:**
- Keep core tight - no sagging
- Alternative: Use the iron beam at a low angle if possible
- Squeeze shoulder blades together at top

### LOWER BODY

#### Bodyweight Squats
**Sets:** 3 | **Reps:** 15 | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Bodyweight Squat](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/bodyweight-squat.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out
- Extend arms forward for balance
- Push hips back and bend knees simultaneously
- Lower until thighs are at least parallel to floor
- Keep chest up and back straight
- Drive through heels to stand

**Key points:**
- Knees should track over toes (not cave inward)
- Go as deep as your mobility allows
- Weight should be on mid-foot to heels, not toes

---

#### Reverse Lunges
**Sets:** 2 | **Reps:** 8 each leg | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Reverse Lunge](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/bodyweight-reverse-lunge.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand tall with feet hip-width apart
- Step backward with one foot
- Lower back knee toward floor (don't let it slam down)
- Front knee stays at 90¬∞ angle, tracking over toes
- Push through front heel to return to standing
- Alternate legs

**Key points:**
- Keep torso upright throughout
- Back knee should nearly touch floor
- Easier on knees than forward lunges

---

#### Glute Bridges
**Sets:** 3 | **Reps:** 12 | **Hold:** 2 sec at top | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Glute Bridge](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/glute-bridge.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Lie on back with knees bent, feet flat on floor
- Feet hip-width apart, heels 6-8 inches from glutes
- Press through heels and squeeze glutes
- Lift hips until body forms straight line from shoulders to knees
- Hold and squeeze glutes at top for 2 seconds
- Lower with control

**Key points:**
- Don't hyperextend lower back at top
- Drive through heels, not toes
- Really squeeze glutes hard at the top

---

#### Single-Leg Calf Raises
**Sets:** 2 | **Reps:** 15 each leg | **Rest:** 45 sec

![Calf Raise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/standing-calf-raise.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand on one foot near a wall for balance
- Rise up onto the ball of your foot as high as possible
- Pause briefly at the top
- Lower slowly with control
- Complete all reps on one leg before switching

**Key points:**
- Full range of motion - heel below toes at bottom
- Go as high as possible at top
- Don't bounce - control the movement

---

### CORE

#### Dead Bug
**Sets:** 2 | **Reps:** 8 each side | **Rest:** 45 sec

![Dead Bug Exercise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/dead-bug.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Lie on back with arms pointed toward ceiling
- Raise legs with knees bent at 90¬∞ (tabletop position)
- Press lower back firmly into floor
- Simultaneously extend opposite arm overhead and opposite leg out
- Leg hovers just above the floor
- Return to start and repeat with other side

**Key points:**
- Lower back MUST stay pressed into floor
- If back arches, don't extend limbs as far
- Breathe out as you extend, in as you return

---

#### Plank
**Sets:** 2 | **Duration:** 20-30 seconds | **Rest:** 45 sec

![Plank Exercise](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/plank-hold.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Forearms on floor, elbows directly under shoulders
- Rise up onto toes, body in straight line
- Engage core by drawing belly button toward spine
- Squeeze glutes to prevent hip sagging
- Keep neck neutral (look at floor, not forward)

**Key points:**
- No sagging hips or piked-up butt
- Breathe normally - don't hold breath
- Quality over duration - stop when form breaks

#### Bulgarian Split Squats ‚≠ê *Key Exercise*
**Sets:** 3 | **Reps:** 8 each leg | **Rest:** 90 sec

![Bulgarian Split Squat](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/bodyweight-bulgarian-split-squat.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand 2-3 feet in front of bed/chair
- Place top of rear foot on the elevated surface
- Front foot flat on ground, far enough forward that knee doesn't pass toes
- Lower body by bending front knee
- Back knee descends toward floor
- Front thigh should reach parallel to floor
- Drive through front heel to stand

**Key points:**
- This is the KING of single-leg exercises
- Keep torso upright or slightly forward
- Front knee tracks over toes
- Expect significant soreness in first week

### Hamstrings & Posterior Chain

#### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift (Bodyweight)
**Sets:** 2 | **Reps:** 10 each leg | **Rest:** 60 sec

![Single Leg RDL](https://www.inspireusafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/single-leg-romanian-deadlift.gif)

**How to perform:**
- Stand on one leg with slight knee bend
- Hinge at the hip, pushing hips backward
- Extend free leg behind you for counterbalance
- Lower torso until nearly parallel to floor
- Keep back flat throughout
- Return to standing by driving hips forward

**Key points:**
- This teaches the hip hinge pattern
- Keep hips square (don't rotate open)
- Use wall or doorframe for balance if needed initially
- Free leg and torso move as one unit

# üìù Important Notes

### On Pull-Up Progression
The negative pull-ups are doing the real work. Don't get frustrated if full pull-ups take time. Going from "can't do a dead hang pull-up" to "3-4 clean reps" in a month is realistic and excellent progress.

### On the Shoulder
If at any point you feel that pinching sensation again:
- Back off pull work for a few days
- Increase the prehab exercises
- Better to lose a few days than a few months

### On Lower Body Soreness
Since you've neglected lower body, expect significant soreness in weeks 1-2, especially from Bulgarian split squats. This is normal and will settle. Your legs will catch up relatively quickly.

### On Rest
With your improved sleep and nutrition, you'll recover well. But if you feel beat up, take an extra rest day. Three quality sessions beat five mediocre ones.

### On Progression After Week 4
- Add a backpack with books for weighted push-ups
- Continue progressing pull-up volume
- Explore pike push-up progressions (feet elevated)
- Add tempo work (slower eccentrics)
- Consider adding resistance bands to water bottle exercises
